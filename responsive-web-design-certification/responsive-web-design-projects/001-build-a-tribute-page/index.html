Debian

Well-known for its stability and "whenever it's ready" release cycle, Debian has remained relevant and popular since its initial release in 1993.

Debian is a non-commercial distribution of Linux maintained by a large international community of users and developers. "It is the only large project with a constitution, social contract, and policy documents to organize the project."

Debian supports many uncommon processor architectures, more than 50,000 software packages, and even alternative non-Linux kernels.

New stable versions are released roughly every two years. Releases can expect three years of support, plus an additional two years of LTS security updates.
